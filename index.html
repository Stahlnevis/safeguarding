About Elimu

Elimu is building a learning community in Kenya empowering vulnerable learners of all kinds in achieving their educational and work-related goals. Over the years, the objectives of Elimu became clearer and the focus has been honed from ‘enhancing access to education’ to ‘providing solutions for vulnerable children, youth, and adults to achieve their life goals at every stage of their learning’.

Organization Background

Elimu is an official charitable organization registered since 2011 in Canada and since 2013 in Kenya. Elimu is built of a team of professionals from different cultural and spiritual backgrounds united by an authentic passion for improving lives by facilitating self-directed learning, especially for those from vulnerable situations. Each of those serving children in Kenya does so from a living Christian faith. Elimu readily serves all faith backgrounds without reservation.

Projects and Initiatives

  • Elimu Resource Centre – a digital hub for learners
  • Artisan Maker Space – boosting income generation for sewing artisans
  • Mobile Digital Reading Program – taking digital resources to vulnerable communities
  • Offline eLearning Solution – based on the Kolibri edutech platform
  • Stay in School Project – keeping Kenyan kids in school
  • Elimu Sewing Project - digital literacy and a sewing trade improve livelihoods for young women

Safeguarding Policy

This policy document has been developed to ensure that staff, interns/volunteers, and partners of Elimu have adequate child safeguarding measures in place for reducing the risk of and for addressing concerns related to child abuse, exploitation, and neglect that can result from personnel.

Legal Framework and Commitment

The legal framework states that all children under the age of 18 have a right to protection from abuse or exploitation. Elimu Development Projects (Elimu) will not tolerate any child abuse or exploitation as it goes against the very beliefs, values, and aims of our organization. We have a responsibility to ensure that all employees, volunteers, consultants, and partners are given clear guidance about the standards of behavior and practice required of them at all times when they are in contact with children.

Policy Update and Accountability

This policy forms part of an employee's terms and conditions of employment and may be subject to change at the discretion of management. We continually monitor and update our safeguarding policies to be accountable and improve. We believe we have a moral obligation to maintain the highest ethical standards in our work.

Commitments

Elimu commits to treat children and young people with dignity and respect in all areas of its work. It takes all reasonable measures to control known and foreseeable risks to children and young people involved in its activities.

Elimu’s Child protection Policy reflects good practice among international standards of working with children. Elimu commits to encourage children and young people, wherever appropriate, to contribute to decisions affecting them.

Elimu commits to respect every child regardless of race, colour, gender, sexual orientation, language, religion, political or other opinion; national, ethnic or social origin, property, disability, birth or other status. This commitment is consistent with the United Nations Child values.

Elimu commits to act in all situations, in the best interest of the needs of the child.

Elimu will ensure that every member of staff understands their responsibilities in child protection, including the responsibility to behave appropriately when working with children and young people.

Elimu ensures that staff with management responsibility for activities involving direct contact with children and young people are sensitized to communicate child protection measures to their subordinates.

Partners working with Elimu must either have their own child protection policy or will abide by Elimu’s policy.
